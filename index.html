SummerTechLive Studentship: Natural Language Processing
Summer 2016-17

Research and development project in biomedical natural language processing (NLP), completed on location with an industry partner. Myself and another student, with limited supervision, developed a Java software program, with data extracted using the NCBI E-utilities API, abstracts parsed using the Stanford Parser, and key terms identified using PubTator. The system analysed syntactic dependencies to determine the claims of treatment effects, achieving precision and recall close to the state of the art of similar systems at the time.

AMSI Vacation Research Scholarship: Theoretical Statistics
Summer 2015-16

Theoretical statistics research project comparing two approaches to producing linear mixed models with time varying co-variates, showing that claims, of a 2011 paper, of improvement over traditional methods, may have been incorrect.
